The story

Will Turner has spent more than 20 years in rooms with leaders — on stage, in boardrooms, in coaching conversations at 7am before the day begins. And in every one of those rooms, he has been doing the same thing: finding the simple human truth inside the unconscious complexity, and using it to move people forward.

He didn't always have a name for it. But a friend called Helen — in London, 15 years ago — looked at him and said: "You are all about simplexity — making complex things simple. It is a real gift."

Two independent coaching sessions later, the same word surfaced again. From different people, different contexts, different countries. The same truth. So he named it. Simplexity.

And then something even more important happened. He identified the enemy: unconscious complexity — the complexity leaders create beyond what the situation actually requires, without knowing they're doing it. And he built the framework to show them where it lives — and how to move through the penny drop to the other side.
